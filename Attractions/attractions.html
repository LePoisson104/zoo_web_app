Attractions

Cougar Exhibit

Shasta & Shasha

  • The cougar is known by many names including panther, puma, and mountain lion. Cougars have the largest range of any mammals in the Western Hemisphere and live in deserts, mountains, and rainforests.

Rides

  • Cougar Roller Coaster

American Alligator Exhibit

Ally, Gatorade, and Chomper

  • In the 1960s, the American alligator's numbers were so low that it was placed on the endangered species list. Luckily, this species has made a monumental recovery and is currently listed by the IUCN as a species of Least Concern.

Rides

  • Alligator Swing

Capybara Exhibit

Chico & Coco

  • The capybara is the largest rodent in the world! It is related to the guinea pig, but capybara are six times as large and weigh 50 times as much.

Rides

  • Capybara Carousel

Asian Elephant Exhibit

Whopper, Chunky, and Floppy

  • These majestic creatures are struggling to survive in the wild. Houston Zoo partners with field conservation organizations to ensure elephants will exist for a long time to come.

Rides

  • Elephant Drop

Orangutan Exhibit

Gonzo, Chuckles, Beans, and Goofy

  • Orangutan means “man of the forest” in Malay and Indonesian languages. Orangutans are the only one of the four great apes (orangutan, gorilla, chimpanzee, and bonobo) not native to Africa; they live in the southeast asian islands of Borneo and Sumatra.

Rides

  • Bumper Cars

California Sea Lion Exhibit

Shorty & Spotty

  • They are called “sea lions” because they produce loud roars like lions. Males from some species even grow thick manes around their necks!

Rides

  • Sea Lion Sky Coaster
